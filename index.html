About This Site

This website is dedicated to telling the story of the epic Indian movie franchise *Baahubali*. Through this site, you will get to explore the journey of two great princes, Amarendra Baahubali and his son, Mahendra Baahubali, as they battle treachery, overcome obstacles, and seek to reclaim the throne of the kingdom of Mahishmati.

The story unfolds across two parts, *Baahubali: The Beginning* and *Baahubali: The Conclusion*, directed by S. S. Rajamouli. The films have captured the hearts of millions with their grand scale, unforgettable characters, and breathtaking action sequences.

Baahubali: The Beginning (2015)

The first part, *Baahubali: The Beginning*, introduces the kingdom of Mahishmati and centers on two main characters:

  • Shivudu (Baahubali): A strong and courageous young man who grows up in a remote village and is unaware of his royal lineage.
  • Bhallaladeva: A ruthless prince, and the son of the king, Bijjaladeva.

Shivudu, while living in the village, becomes curious about the people in the nearby kingdom of Mahishmati. After many adventures, he learns about his real identity as the son of Maharani Sivagami and King Vikramadeva. He is the rightful heir to the throne of Mahishmati.

Meanwhile, Bhallaladeva wants to take the throne by any means necessary. He manipulates his way to power, but the true heir, Baahubali (Shivudu's father), is the hero of the kingdom. Shivudu embarks on a journey to defeat Bhallaladeva and reclaim the throne for the good of the people.

At the end of *Baahubali: The Beginning*, the film ends on a cliffhanger, with Shivudu (Baahubali) learning that his father, Baahubali, was killed under mysterious circumstances, and Shivudu's journey to avenge his father and restore the kingdom is set in motion.

Baahubali: The Conclusion (2017)

The second part, *Baahubali: The Conclusion*, delves into the backstory of the events leading up to the death of Baahubali and the rise of Bhallaladeva.

Through flashbacks, the film reveals how Baahubali (Amarendra Baahubali) was originally chosen to be the king of Mahishmati. Baahubali was loved by the people for his wisdom, valor, and kindness. However, Bhallaladeva, jealous and ambitious, schemes to take over the kingdom.

As the story progresses, Shivudu (Baahubali's son) returns to Mahishmati, confronts Bhallaladeva, and uncovers the truth about his father's death. In the epic climax, Shivudu, now fully embracing his role as Baahubali, leads the people of Mahishmati in a final battle to overthrow Bhallaladeva and reclaim the throne.

The film concludes with Shivudu restoring justice to the kingdom, avenging his father's death, and ensuring that the kingdom is led by rightful and benevolent rulers.

Themes and Elements

  • Royalty and Power: The story revolves around power struggles between royal family members, focusing on the conflict between Baahubali and Bhallaladeva.
  • Loyalty and Betrayal: The characters in the film display varying degrees of loyalty and betrayal, with deep emotional arcs and strong character motivations.
  • Epic Battles: The films are known for their grand action sequences, breathtaking visuals, and large-scale battle scenes.
  • Cinematic Achievements: The films gained worldwide acclaim for their visual effects, direction, and storytelling, becoming a cultural phenomenon.

Key Characters

  • Baahubali (Amarendra Baahubali): The courageous and noble prince who stands as a symbol of righteousness.
  • Bhallaladeva: The villainous prince, driven by ambition and greed.
  • Sivagami: The powerful and wise queen who becomes a central figure in the kingdom's political struggle.
  • Devasena: Baahubali's wife and a warrior princess who plays a key role in the story.
  • Kattappa: The loyal general who has a complex relationship with Baahubali and his family, and whose actions have a significant impact on the plot.

Legacy

"Baahubali" is one of the highest-grossing films in Indian cinema and has been praised for its scale, direction, and visual effects. It became a major cultural touchstone and garnered a large fan following both in India and internationally. The series is also notable for bringing attention to Telugu cinema on a global scale.
